## Overview

This document compiles regulatory requirements applicable to workers compensation insurance model development and documentation. Workers compensation is heavily regulated with state-specific requirements, bureau involvement, and unique statutory benefit structures.

---

## Section 1: Regulatory Structure Overview

### 1.1 State vs. Federal Jurisdiction

**State Authority:**
- Workers compensation is primarily state-regulated
- Each state has its own workers compensation act
- Benefits defined by state statute
- Rates subject to state approval

**Federal Involvement:**
- Longshore and Harbor Workers' Act (federal coverage)
- Federal Employees' Compensation Act
- Jones Act (maritime workers)
- Black Lung Benefits Act (coal miners)

### 1.2 Rating Bureaus

**NCCI States (38 states):**
- National Council on Compensation Insurance
- Files loss costs and rating values
- Maintains classification system
- Administers experience rating plan

**Independent Bureau States:**
- California (WCIRB)
- Delaware (DCRB)
- Indiana (WCCRB)
- Massachusetts (WCRIBMA)
- Michigan (NCCI advisory)
- Minnesota (MWCIA)
- New Jersey (NJCRIB)
- New York (NYCIRB)
- North Carolina (NCRB)
- Pennsylvania (PCRB)
- Wisconsin (WCRB)

**Monopolistic States (state fund only):**
- North Dakota
- Ohio
- Washington
- Wyoming

### 1.3 Residual Market

**Assigned Risk Pool:**
- Coverage for employers unable to obtain voluntary market
- NCCI administers in most states
- Higher rates than voluntary market
- Servicing carrier arrangements

## Section 2: NCCI Requirements

### 2.1 Classification System

**Governing Classification:**
- Employer assigned to classification based on operations
- One governing class per policy (with exceptions)
- Standard exception classifications (clerical, outside sales)
- Classification guide maintained by NCCI

**Classification Accuracy:**
- Insurers must properly classify risks
- Audit verification of classification
- Misclassification subject to penalties
- Appeals process for classification disputes

### 2.2 Experience Rating Plan

**Plan Structure:**
- Applies to risks meeting premium threshold
- Uses three years of payroll and loss data
- Separates primary (frequency) from excess (severity) losses
- Produces experience modification factor (mod)

**Formula Components:**
- Expected losses (based on class and payroll)
- Actual losses (capped at split point)
- Credibility weighting (based on expected losses)
- Mod = Actual weighted / Expected weighted

**Data Requirements:**
- Unit Statistical Plan data reporting
- Loss data reported by claim
- Payroll data reported by class
- Timing: annual filing by specified date

### 2.3 Loss Cost Filing

**NCCI Loss Cost Process:**
- Annual loss cost filing by state
- Based on industry aggregate experience
- Includes trend and development factors
- Does not include expense or profit

**Insurer Responsibilities:**
- Develop loss cost multiplier (LCM)
- File LCM with state (if required)
- Justify deviation from bureau
- Maintain supporting data

### 2.4 Unit Statistical Plan

**Reporting Requirements:**
- All policies reported to NCCI
- Policy-level and claim-level data
- Standardized data elements
- Annual reporting deadlines

**Data Elements:**
- Policy information (effective date, premium, payroll by class)
- Claim information (claim number, injury date, injury type)
- Loss amounts (paid, reserved, incurred)
- Claim status (open, closed)

## Section 4: State Benefit Structures

### 4.1 Benefit Types

**Medical Benefits:**
- Unlimited in most states
- Fee schedule limitations (state-specific)
- Utilization review requirements
- Pharmacy benefit management

**Indemnity Benefits:**

*Temporary Total Disability (TTD):*
- Percentage of pre-injury wage (typically 66 2/3%)
- Subject to state maximum
- Duration until maximum medical improvement

*Permanent Partial Disability (PPD):*
- Scheduled injuries (specific body part)
- Unscheduled injuries (whole body impairment)
- Wage loss vs. impairment-based (varies by state)

*Permanent Total Disability (PTD):*
- Lifetime benefits in most states
- COLA adjustments in some states
- Second injury fund offsets

*Death Benefits:*
- Percentage of wage to dependents
- Funeral expenses
- Duration limits vary by state

### 4.2 Benefit Level Monitoring

**Actuarial Considerations:**
- Annual benefit level changes
- Maximum weekly wage tracking
- COLA provisions
- Legislative benefit changes

**Documentation Requirements:**
- Current benefit levels by state
- Scheduled benefit increases
- Impact on severity projections
- Trend assumptions

### 5.1 Fee Schedules

**State Fee Schedule Types:**
- Resource-Based Relative Value Scale (RBRVS)
- Percentage of Medicare
- Own fee schedule
- No fee schedule (minority of states)

**Fee Schedule Updates:**
- Annual update process
- Effective date timing
- Conversion factor changes
- New procedure code additions

### 5.2 Utilization Review

**Requirements:**
- Pre-authorization for certain procedures
- Concurrent review during treatment
- Retrospective review
- Treatment guidelines (ODG, ACOEM)

### 5.3 Pharmacy Regulation

**Drug Formularies:**
- State-mandated formularies (some states)
- Prior authorization requirements
- Generic substitution
- Compound medication restrictions

**Pharmacy Benefit Management:**
- PBM contracting
- Network requirements
- Dispensing limits
- Opioid restrictions (increasingly common)

## Section 6: Rate Regulation

### 6.1 Filing Requirements

**Loss Cost States:**
- Bureau files loss costs
- Insurer files loss cost multiplier
- LCM includes expenses and profit
- Supporting actuarial documentation

**Rate States:**
- Bureau files advisory rates
- Insurer can deviate (up or down)
- Deviation filing required
- Competitive rating allowed

### 6.2 Actuarial Memorandum Requirements

**Standard Components:**
- Data description and quality
- Development factors and selection
- Trend analysis
- Rate indication calculation
- Implementation plan

**Workers Comp Specific:**
- Medical vs. indemnity split
- Benefit level adjustments
- Large loss loading
- Experience rating off-balance

### 6.3 Rate Approval Process

**Prior Approval:**
- File before implementation
- State review period
- Potential hearing
- Approval, modification, or denial

**File and Use:**
- File with state
- Implement immediately
- Subject to later review
- Potential rollback
