## Overview

This document compiles regulatory requirements applicable to commercial auto insurance model development and documentation. Commercial auto bridges personal lines and commercial lines regulation, with additional requirements for motor carriers and specialized operations.

---

## Section 1: Regulatory Framework

### 1.1 State Insurance Regulation

**Rate Regulation:**
- Commercial auto subject to state rate regulation
- Most states: competitive rating (file and use)
- Some states: prior approval for rate changes
- Advisory organization filings (ISO)

**Form Regulation:**
- Policy forms subject to state approval
- ISO Commercial Auto policy widely used
- Manuscript forms require filing
- Endorsement approval process

### 1.2 Federal Motor Carrier Regulation

**FMCSA Requirements (For-Hire Carriers):**
- Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration
- Minimum liability limits for interstate carriers
- Financial responsibility filings (MCS-90, BMC-91)
- Operating authority requirements

**Applicability:**
- Interstate for-hire motor carriers
- Vehicles over 10,000 lbs GVW
- Hazardous materials transporters
- Passenger carriers (buses)

### 1.3 ISO and Advisory Organizations

**ISO Commercial Auto:**
- Loss cost filings by state
- Classification system (vehicle type, size, use)
- Territory definitions
- Increased limit factors

**NCCI Interface:**
- Workers compensation interaction
- Fleet experience data
- Classification alignment

## Section 3: Motor Carrier Requirements

### 3.1 Federal Financial Responsibility

**Minimum Liability Limits:**

| Carrier Type | Commodity | Minimum Limit |
|-------------|-----------|---------------|
| For-hire (general freight) | Non-hazardous | $750,000 |
| For-hire (general freight) | Hazardous | $1,000,000 - $5,000,000 |
| For-hire | Oil | $1,000,000 |
| For-hire | Passengers (≤15) | $1,500,000 |
| For-hire | Passengers (>15) | $5,000,000 |

### 3.2 Insurance Filings

**MCS-90 Endorsement:**
- Required for interstate motor carriers
- Ensures minimum liability limits
- Applies regardless of policy terms
- Cannot be cancelled without FMCSA notice

**BMC-91 (Certificate of Insurance):**
- Filed with FMCSA
- Certifies coverage meets requirements
- Cancellation notice requirements (30 days)
- Reinstatement procedures

**BMC-91X (Surety Bond):**
- Alternative to insurance
- Filed with FMCSA
- Same minimum limits

### 3.3 Operating Authority

**MC Number:**
- Motor Carrier number assigned by FMCSA
- Required for interstate for-hire carriers
- Verify operating authority status
- Check Safety Measurement System (SMS) scores

**USDOT Number:**
- Required for interstate commerce
- Vehicle identification requirement
- Displayed on commercial vehicles

## Section 5: Coverage Requirements

### 5.1 Liability Coverage

**Standard Coverages:**
- Bodily injury liability
- Property damage liability
- Combined single limit (CSL) option

**Required Coverages:**
- Uninsured motorist (state requirements)
- Underinsured motorist (state requirements)
- Personal injury protection (no-fault states)
- Medical payments (optional but common)

### 5.2 Physical Damage

**Collision:**
- Coverage for collision with object or vehicle
- Deductible options
- Actual cash value vs. stated amount

**Comprehensive:**
- Non-collision physical damage
- Theft, vandalism, weather
- Glass coverage

### 5.3 Specialized Coverages

**Hired and Non-Owned Auto:**
- Employees using personal vehicles
- Rented or borrowed vehicles
- Liability only (no physical damage)

**Motor Truck Cargo:**
- Coverage for goods being transported
- Separate policy or endorsement
- Carrier legal liability

**Garage Coverage:**
- Auto dealers
- Repair shops
- Parking facilities

## Section 7: Experience and Schedule Rating

### 7.1 Experience Rating Plans

**ISO Commercial Auto Experience Rating:**
- Applies to accounts meeting threshold
- Three-year experience period
- Credibility-weighted
- Produces modification factor

**Plan Components:**
- Expected losses (by class/territory)
- Actual losses (capped)
- Credibility formula
- Off-balance considerations

### 7.2 Schedule Rating

**Purpose:**
- Recognize non-quantifiable risk factors
- Underwriter judgment
- Competitive flexibility

**Rating Factors:**
- Management capability
- Safety programs
- Driver selection/training
- Fleet age and condition
- Financial stability

**Documentation Requirements:**
- Maximum credit/debit limits
- Factor definitions
- Underwriter guidelines
- Audit trail

## Section 10: Residual Market

### 10.1 Assigned Risk

**Availability:**
- For risks unable to obtain voluntary coverage
- Higher rates than voluntary market
- Limited coverage options
- State-administered plans

**Eligibility:**
- Documented rejections from voluntary market
- Minimum required rejections (typically 2-3)
- Application process

### 10.2 Facility/Pool Mechanisms

**Automobile Insurance Plans:**
- State-mandated availability
- Shared market mechanism
- Carrier participation based on market share
- Loss sharing provisions
